Honors Thesis — Energy Workforce Development

Overview

For my Barrett Honors Thesis, I partnered with ASU’s Laboratory for Energy and Power Solutions (LEAPS) to build scalable learning modules aimed at preparing the future workforce for the rapidly evolving energy sector. The project included curriculum design, pilot testing, feedback loops, and a full thesis documenting instructional approach, learner insights, and content deployment strategy.

What I Built

  • Developed micro-courses covering wind energy, electric vehicles, and modern energy regulations.
  • Created a structured pilot to test the educational content across a representative learner group.
  • Collected and synthesized feedback to refine learning modules and improve engagement.
  • Authored the thesis detailing the instructional design process, methodology, and outcomes.

Key Outcomes

  • Delivered high-quality educational modules aligned with LEAPS’ mission for workforce upskilling.
  • Received excellent feedback from pilot participants on clarity and instructional design.
  • Produced a complete academic thesis documenting the research and development process.
